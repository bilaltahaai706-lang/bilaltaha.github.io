Research

Graduate Research · University of Texas at Arlington
Heat Sink Performance Enhancement via Topology Optimization
The University of Texas at Arlington – Department of Mechanical Engineering

Investigated the application of topology optimization to redesign heat sink geometries for improved thermal performance. The research focused on reducing bulk heater temperatures by generating structurally efficient and thermally optimized fin geometries. Validated designs through ANSYS thermal simulations and compared results against conventional heat sink configurations.

Key outcomes included a measurable reduction in operating temperatures and weight savings compared to baseline designs, demonstrating the potential of computational optimization in thermal management engineering.

Undergraduate Research · King Saud University
Mechanical Characterization of Cu–Fe Composite Sheathed MgB₂ Superconductive Wire
King Saud University – Materials & Mechanical Engineering

Conducted research on the development of MgB₂ superconductive wires using a composite Cu–Fe sheath to eliminate the need for intermediate strain-relieving annealing processes. The Cu outer sheath was utilized for efficient heat dissipation, while the Fe inner sheath provided structural containment of the MgB₂ powder.

Mechanical properties including elastic modulus, ultimate tensile strength, yield strength, hardness, and microstructure were systematically characterized at different stages of the wire drawing process. Results were compared against MgB₂ wire with a single Fe sheath, demonstrating the superior ductile behavior of the composite configuration.
